1969

The first version of the Internet, ARPANET, is developed in the United States, allowing computers to communicate over long distances.

1983

The Internet adopts TCP/IP, enabling different networks to connect into one global system.

1991

The World Wide Web is created by Tim Berners-Lee, making the Internet accessible to the public.

1997

SixDegrees launches, widely considered the first recognizable social media platform.

2003

Platforms like MySpace and LinkedIn are created, expanding online social interaction.

2004

Facebook is launched by Mark Zuckerberg as a university networking site.

2005

YouTube is created, allowing users to upload and share videos globally.

2006

Twitter launches, introducing short-form messaging (tweets).

2010

Instagram launches, focusing on photo and video sharing.

2011

Snapchat is released, introducing disappearing messages.

2016–17

TikTok (originally Douyin in China) launches internationally in 2017, becoming highly popular among young users.

2021

The Australian government introduces the Online Safety Act 2021, strengthening regulations on harmful online content.

2023–2024

Australia increases discussions and proposals to restrict or regulate social media use for children under certain ages due to safety concerns.
